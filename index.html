About

Recent Publications

Selected publications and research highlights.

Instrumentation · 2019–2021

Developing a Streamlined Instrument for Synchrotron Users

A multi-year collaboration between Los Alamos National Laboratory and Argonne National Laboratory to design and commission a moveable Si(111) double-bounce x-ray monochromator at the 34-ID-C end station. The instrument lets users switch seamlessly between a polychromatic Laue beam, for indexing crystalline grains, and a monochromatic Bragg beam, for reconstructing their three-dimensional strain fields in a single experiment. Presented at TMS 2021.

Quantum Devices · 2015–2018

Improving Quantum Dot Heterostructures

Combined coherent x-ray nanobeam scattering with mechanical-model simulations to accurately measure the electrode-induced strain at the depth of a two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) in GaAs/AlGaAs quantum-dot devices, and quantified how the resulting deformation and piezoelectric potentials reshape the electronic properties of a model quantum-dot device.

Publication · NPG Asia Mater. 2019

Room-Temperature Giant Magnetostriction in Single-Crystal Nickel Nanowires

Using Bragg coherent diffraction imaging at Argonne's 34-ID-C end station, we discovered an unexpectedly large room-temperature magnetostriction in single-crystal Ni nanowires, imaged the three-dimensional nanoscale volumetric strain, and identified the underlying physical mechanism. A. Pateras et al., NPG Asia Materials 11, 59 (2019).
