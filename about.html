About Hi, I'm Anastasios

Ph.D. Physicist and Research Scientist

I am a physicist specializing in coherent x-ray scattering, electron diffraction, and materials characterization. With advanced training from leading research institutions and expertise in both experimental and computational methods, I am passionate about solving complex scientific challenges through rigorous research and data-driven analysis.

How I Got Here

I earned my Ph.D. in Physics from Aix-Marseille University in France, specializing in Materials Science and Image Processing. My academic training included advanced studies in high-energy physics and materials characterization techniques. Since then, I have worked at some of the world's most prestigious research institutions, including the Free Electron Laser Science Center at DESY (Hamburg), Carnegie Mellon University, Los Alamos National Laboratory, and the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

My research career has been defined by cutting-edge experimental work at major synchrotron facilities, particularly Argonne National Laboratory's 34-ID-C beamline. I have led complex experiments, discovered important physical phenomena (such as giant magnetostriction in nickel nanowires), and published my findings in prestigious peer-reviewed journals including Science, Nature, and various top-tier physics publications.

Core Skills & Technical Expertise

Experimental Physics

Expert in time-resolved coherent x-ray diffraction, Bragg coherent diffraction imaging (BCDI), x-ray reflectivity, and coherent electron beam diffraction (CBED). Proficient in designing sophisticated scattering experiments and operating at synchrotron beamlines under demanding conditions.

Data Analysis & Visualization

Advanced Python proficiency for scientific computing, data analysis, and visualization. Experience with PyTorch, machine learning algorithms for image processing, gradient-based optimization, and multivariable optimization methods. Skilled in implementing complex computational workflows for large-scale datasets.

Materials Characterization

Specialized expertise in characterizing ferroelectric materials, quantum dots, heterostructures, nanowires, and other complex materials. Skilled in interpreting scattering data, modeling diffraction patterns, and extracting meaningful physical insights from experimental results.

Research Leadership

Experienced in leading research projects, mentoring junior researchers, and collaborating with international teams. Strong publication record with 25+ peer-reviewed papers. Proven ability to secure funding and manage research initiatives from conception to completion.
